Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 14, 2026

1. Introduction

Project Odysseus ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our tutoring services.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Information: Name, email address, phone number, and student grade level when you contact us or book a session.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you interact with our website, including pages visited and time spent.
  • Device Information: Browser type, IP address, and device type for analytics purposes.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use collected information to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and schedule tutoring sessions
  • Provide and improve our tutoring services
  • Send you updates about your bookings
  • Analyze website usage to improve user experience
  • Comply with legal obligations

4. Information Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share information only with service providers who assist us in operating our website (such as form processing services), and only to the extent necessary to provide our services.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate security measures to protect your personal information. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Your Rights (POPIA Compliance)

Under the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Object to the processing of your information
  • Withdraw consent at any time

7. Cookies

Our website may use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience and analyze website traffic. You can control cookie settings through your browser preferences.

8. Analytics (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics (GA4) to understand how visitors use our website (for example: which pages are visited, which links are clicked, and general usage patterns). This helps us improve the website and our services.

  • What we collect: limited usage data such as pages viewed, approximate location (derived from IP), device/browser information, and link click events.
  • When it loads: we only load Google Analytics after you opt in via the cookie banner.
  • Cookies: Google Analytics may set cookies on your device if you accept analytics cookies.
  • Performance & reliability: if you opt in, we may also collect Web Vitals (e.g. LCP/CLS/INP) and basic error events to help us improve site performance and reliability.
  • Your choice: you can accept or decline analytics cookies using the cookie banner. If you decline, we do not enable analytics.
  • Do Not Track: if your browser has “Do Not Track” enabled, we treat that as a preference to decline analytics by default.
  • Change your mind: you can clear your browser site data (cookies/local storage) to see the banner again and choose differently.
